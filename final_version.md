## Area of Interest

I am deeply interested in the rapid evolution of AI and its impact on software development — specifically, how generative AI is changing what it means to be a programmer. As AI coding assistants become increasingly capable, traditional developers face a shifting landscape. I want to understand both the opportunities this creates and the challenges it poses for engineers entering the field today.

## Three Ways Generative AI Can Be Useful in This Area

- **Accelerating code generation and prototyping:** Generative AI tools can draft boilerplate code, suggest implementations, and rapidly produce working prototypes, allowing developers to focus on higher-level design and problem-solving rather than syntax.
- **Supporting code review and debugging:** AI assistants can analyze existing codebases, identify potential bugs, explain unfamiliar code, and suggest fixes — acting as an always-available pair programmer that reduces review cycles.
- **Lowering the barrier to entry for new developers:** By translating natural language instructions into functional code, generative AI enables people with limited programming backgrounds to build software, potentially democratizing development while also raising questions about skill depth and code quality.
